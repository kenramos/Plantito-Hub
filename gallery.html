Bird's nest

Grows in a series of erect, spoon-shaped, and apple-colored fronds that rise from a central rosette. These are beautiful plants that require a bit of babying to reach their fullest potential.

Pink Chinese Evergreen

A leafy plant that thrives in tropical forests or other areas of high humidity, partial light, and moist soil. A common houseplant, which flourishes in shady gardens or near humid areas of a home such as a kitchen or bath.

Coleus

Coleus have some of the most stunningly colored foliage—in combinations of green, yellow, pink, red, maroon, etc. No matter what area you are looking to put coleus, you can find one that will be perfect.

Pink Coleus

Easy-to-grow coleus plants aren’t just for shade anymore. Heat and sun-tolerant varieties are widely available, making them a popular choice for many areas, and their bold and beautiful foliage make them the center of attention no matter where they’re planted.

Peperomia Amigo Marcello

This plant has all the features to look for in a good houseplant: They tolerate a range of growing conditions, sport interesting foliage, and stay relatively small.

Ficus Nana

One of the most widely used in ornamental horticulture, it has a great value for its decorative leaves and the overall shape of the plant that makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Fittonia

A compact plant with striking patterned foliage. Fittonia are fairly easy to care for–they can tolerate a range of lighting conditions and like to be kept moist.

Green Fittonia

This houseplant craves high humidity but not too much irrigation. This little beauty does well in terrariums, hanging baskets, dish gardens or even as a ground cover in the right climate.

Silver Pothos

Silver Satin Pothos likes bright, indirect light year round. Harsh, direct sunlight will scorch the leaves, while too little light will cause the leaves to lose their variegation.

Jade Plant + Fittonia + Zebra Plant

This trio of plants is ready for the taking.

Monstera

Monsteras are famous for their natural leaf-holes, and has led to the rise of its nickname, Swiss Cheese Plant.

Oxalis

Often referred to as “purple shamrocks”, they open and close not just their blooms, but also their leaves in response to light. Oxalis are low-maintenance houseplants that thrive with just minimal care

Peace Lily

A long-time favorite of those with a green-thumb and even those without, the peace lily, is an adaptable and low-maintenance houseplant.The showy part of the flower features a white, hoodlike sheath (known as a spathe) which resembles a white flag of surrender.

Peperomia

Has pale, thick, silvery green round medium-sized leaves marked by heavy veining in shades of deep green or purple running in bands the length of the leaves. The red stems of the Peperomia Albovittata are succulent and fleshy.

Pilea Repens

Not as popular as the other pilea varieties, this specimen is really easy to grow from seeds and offers fantastic foliage that’s a mix of deep green and black.

Sansevieria Starfish

Starfish Sansevieria are rare but worth searching for. They are a more compact hybrid of the common snake plant and has fan-shaped light green foliage with dark green concentric circles from the top to the bottom of the leaf.

Spider Plant

Considered one of the most adaptable of houseplants and the easiest to grow. This plant can grow in a wide range of conditions and suffers from few problems, other than brown tips.

Wandering Jew

Much loved for their unique bright colors and their vining growth habit, the wandering jew looks gorgeous in hanging baskets, or set atop a pedestal, where the tendrils can cascade down.

Birkin

A rare gorgeous houseplant with dark green leaves. The beautiful leaves are variegated with a light yellow too. Birkins stand out from the other houseplants and bring creativity to your home.

Golden Pothos

One of the easiest houseplants to take care of. The Golden Pothos is known for its heart-shaped green leaves with variegation in yellow and is particularly fitting for indoor settings because it is able to filter gaseous toxins like formaldehyde from the air.
